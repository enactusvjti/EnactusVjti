About

Foot Operated Door Opener {FODO}

A Product designed to reduce possibilities of the Corona Virus outbreak.

Purpose

One of the products in the project Nirmal is foot operated door opener. The public places like public toilets, schools, colleges are the most vulnerable places for the spread of the pandemic because thousands of people touch the same door handles leading to transmission of viruses in the community rapidly. So, the FODO has become the necessity of the human kind. It is easy to use and affordable to the people.

Product Specification

  • Model name: Foot Operated Door Opener (FODO)
  • Install- Door mounted at bottom Corner
  • Waterproof, no Electronic component involved

  • Easy to install

  • Material- Steel

  • Color-Dark powder coat (Black/Brown/Silver)

  • Safe and reliable Spring locking and unlocking system.
  • Operation- Foot operated locking and unlocking, "no hand contact involved"
  • Designed By- Enactus VJTI ; Dhaval Diwani ; Sumit Patil

Impact

Avoids Community spread of virus

  • Eliminates 100% hand to handle germ transfer.

  • The people will use their legs and hence hand contact is avoided.

  • By using feet we make sure that the contact to our vital parts that are susceptible to virus doesn't reach, avoiding further transmission of virus till some extent.

Reduce waste

  • It reduce our Carbon Footprint by eliminating the wasteful use of paper towels when existing the restroom, toilets.

  • Many people use additional paper towels to open the dirty door handle when there is NO such mechanism, hence it reduces waste and paper towel costs.

Reduce expense

  • In India, rural people in slums spend at least Rs 100 each year for the treatment of water/sanitation-related diseases.

  • According to the Government of India, this adds up to number of crores annually, which is just little less than the annual budget of the Union health ministry’s and more than the allocation for education.

  • Once fitted no further cost – very low maintenance.

Employment

  • We are well aware about the effects of COVID-19 on the jobs of daily wages workers

  • There are about 40 million informal workers, who are casual or daily wage workers involved in vulnerable occupations in urban areas, who may not get back their employment or livelihood status for a longer period in the near future and are going to be trapped in deeper poverty.

  • A new company can be formed with the help of Government which can employ thousands of unemployed people
